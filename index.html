Moutain Bikes

Mountain biking doesn't mean you need to be biking in a mountain. Any kind of biking in the wilderness can be considered mountain biking. There are different types of mountain bikes, each suited to a certain discipline within the sport. The most commonly known one is a full suspension bike. This has suspension on both the front and rear wheel allowing you to maintain traction in all kinds of trail conditions.

Road Bikes

These bikes are mainly used for paved surfaces. These bikes are designed to put the rider in an aerodynamic riding position. They also have low rolling resistance tires and lower gear ratios that allow the rider to go fast... like 40+ mph :O!!! As with mountain bikes, these bikes also have different types. Unlike mountain bikes that have a distinct style/capability for a trail, road bikes follow similar designs as they are meant to be used in the same riding conditions. There is the regular "raod bike" and a gravel bike.

Hybrid Bikes

Hybrid bikes are a combination of mountain bikes and road bikes. They combine the speed and functionality of a road bike and the style and comfort of a mountain bike. This way the user is comfortable riding the bike for communiting while also having the ability to take care of different gear ratios to tackle different obastacles.

Speciality Bikes

Speciality bikes are bikes that are unique for a certain task. Some common types of speciality bikes are beach crusiers, unicycles, and fixies. Beach crusiers have 1 gear ratio that is appropiate for riding on the beach. They have wider tires to allow you to ride on the sand. Unicycles are just for those CRAZY PEOPLE. You need an immense amount of control over your balance and skill to help you balance. Fixies are also known as track bikes. They are used mainly on indoor circuits as the pedas are fixed to the rear tire. This means that the pedals turn with the rear tire. It won't stop when you stop pedaling!

Mountain Bikes

As mentioned before, mountain bikes are mainly used for biking in the wilderness. They can double down and be used on the road but this is not reccommended as the tires are soft and can do unexpected things on the road that may lead to an accident. They also wear much faster on the pavement than on the trails. Mountain biking can also be a few hours long or can become a journey known as "bike packing." Both activies are a lot of fun for people of all ages.

Racing

In nearly every form of transportation created, mankind has created some form of a race associated with it. Bikes are no exception. There are plenty of races in both mountain biking and road biking. One of the most notable races in the world for cycling is the Tour de France. They use road bikes in that race. It is fun to watch at first but may get old after a while if you are not into the sport. The olympics have a bunch of sports in the summer regarding the different kinds of racing, including track racing! Track racing is just indoor racing on a track that looks similar a track but is curved and made of wood. Fixies are used to race on them.

E-Bikes

E-bikes are bicycles but have a motor as a part of the drivetrain. There are pedal assist e-bikes which assist the user when they are pedaling. There are also full on drive bicycles where the motor will power the bike itself like an electric motorcycle. These bikes provide additional assistance to the rider which make them illegal to use in any kind of race. They also go too fast which is why they are banned on certain bike paths or mountain trails. They are dangerous because of their speed but if used correctly, they can extend the range a rider can go which opens a load of possibilities and adventures.

Road Bikes on the Trail

Most road bikes are not even capable of doing a basic fire trail (a smooth path in the mountain to allow fire-fighting equipment to fight fires). There is the "gravel bike" which puts wider towers and a less aggressive riding position. It usually has no suspension but can be found with suspension on the fork in rare cases. This bike is ONLY meant for light gravel areas and should not be ridden as a mountain bike!

Hybrid Bikes

These bikes are a cross between road bikes and mountain bikes. They often have suspension on the fork if they are more "mountain" oriented or no suspension if it is more "commuter" oriented. These bikes combine both of these worlds to make a bike that can't really do mountain trails nor go fast on the road. But they are very comfortable and are a great bike for those that don't want to invest thousands into a bike.
